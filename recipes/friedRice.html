Chicken Fried Rice Recipe


Photo by Dotdash Meredith Food Studios

Description

This chicken fried rice recipe is a quick and easy meal that can be whipped up in under 30 minutes. It's packed with flavor and perfect for using up leftover rice and vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, diced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: sliced green onions for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the beaten eggs and scramble until fully cooked. Remove the eggs from the skillet and set aside.
  2. Add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil to the skillet. Saut√© the chopped onion and minced garlic until fragrant and translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the diced cooked chicken to the skillet and cook for another 2-3 minutes until heated through.
  4. Add the frozen peas and carrots to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes until they are tender.
  5. Add the cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir everything together to combine.
  6. Pour the soy sauce over the rice mixture and stir well to evenly distribute the sauce. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, allowing the rice to slightly crisp up on the bottom.
  7. Return the scrambled eggs to the skillet and mix them into the rice. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Remove from heat and garnish with sliced green onions if desired. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade chicken fried rice!
